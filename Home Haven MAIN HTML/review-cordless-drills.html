How to Choose the Right Cordless Drill

Before buying any drill, consider how often you'll use it and what kinds of jobs you'll tackle. Occasional furniture assembly calls for something different than weekend renovation projects.

Voltage: 18–20V drills handle most household jobs comfortably. 12V drills are lighter but less powerful. Only go below 18V if weight is your top priority.

Brushless vs Brushed: Brushless motors last longer and run more efficiently, but cost more. For occasional use, brushed is fine. For frequent use, brushless is worth the investment.

Battery ecosystem: If you already own tools from DeWalt, Milwaukee, or Ryobi, staying in that ecosystem means your batteries are interchangeable — a significant money saver over time.
