How to Choose an Accent Chair for a Small Room

Before buying, measure your space carefully. A good rule of thumb is leaving at least 18 inches between your accent chair and other furniture for comfortable movement. Here are the key things to consider:

📐 Size & Scale
Look for chairs under 30 inches wide for small rooms. Slim arms and legs visually open up the space compared to bulkier designs.
🎨 Color & Fabric
Lighter fabrics make small rooms feel bigger. Velvet adds luxury but shows wear faster. Linen and boucle are great for everyday use.
🦵 Leg Style
Chairs with exposed legs make a room feel more open because you can see the floor beneath them. Avoid fully skirted chairs in tight spaces.
🛠️ Durability
Check the frame material. Solid wood and metal frames outlast particleboard. Look for chairs with at least 4-star reviews mentioning long-term use.
